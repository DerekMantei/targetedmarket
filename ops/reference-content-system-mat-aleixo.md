## Core model observed

The creator is not selling isolated videography. The business is framed as a system that turns creative production into strategic infrastructure. The public funnel connects four layers:

1. Personal creator brand
   - Documents lifestyle, building, travel, taste, and entrepreneurial progress.
   - Creates trust and aspiration before selling.

2. Educational content
   - Converts operating knowledge into numbered, highly visual lessons.
   - Teaches specific process improvements rather than vague motivation.

3. Productized system
   - Names and packages the operating method.
   - Positions the offer as an installation or infrastructure, not a random service.

4. Premium agency proof
   - Portfolio, case studies, testimonials, and discovery calls support the premium positioning.

## Visual language from Derek's screenshots

- Vertical full-screen Reel format.
- Dark, warm, cinematic environments.
- Premium offices, industrial interiors, travel, clothing, and equipment used as atmospheric B-roll.
- Large white sans-serif headings.
- Small uppercase category label above the heading.
- One numbered operating lesson per frame.
- Short supporting paragraph with a concrete business outcome.
- Screenshot or mockup of the actual document/system being discussed.
- Minimal movement; the background carries mood while text carries information.
- CTA appears at the end: comment a keyword to receive a resource.

The visual is not the real advantage. The advantage is that every Reel makes an invisible business process tangible.

## Content formula

### Frame 1 — problem or promise

Use a clear business pain:
- leads disappear after the first conversation
- nobody follows up on promised statements
- an application is submitted but the merchant never activates
- agents are recruited without measurable activity standards

### Frames 2–5 — numbered system

Each frame should include:
- CATEGORY LABEL
- NUMBERED STEP
- one action
- one reason it matters
- a visual artifact: CRM screen, checklist, email, call log, statement diagram, onboarding timeline, or equipment workflow

### Final frame — one CTA

Examples:
- Comment `STATEMENT` for the review checklist.
- Comment `CALL` for the merchant opener.
- Comment `PIPELINE` for the follow-up stages.
- Comment `AGENT` for the field-ready scorecard.

Do not offer a lead magnet that does not exist. Do not use automated Instagram DMs until the consent and fulfillment workflow is tested.

## First Reel series to produce

Series title: THE MERCHANT ONBOARDING SYSTEM

### Reel 1 — Before the call

Frames:
1. RESEARCH — Know what the business sells and how customers pay.
2. CONTACT — Identify the likely decision maker and public business number.
3. PURPOSE — Enter with one reason for the conversation, not a generic rate pitch.
4. CRM — Create the lead before calling so the result has a place to go.
5. NEXT ACTION — No lead exists without a date for what happens next.

CTA: Comment `CALL` for the merchant-call checklist.

### Reel 2 — After they send the statement

Frames:
1. SECURE HANDLING — Keep sensitive information out of public tools.
2. EFFECTIVE RATE — Total fees divided by total processed volume.
3. OPERATIONS — Review equipment, deposits, support, and transaction mix.
4. OPTIONS — Compare only suitable, approved programs.
5. REVIEW — Present the findings clearly; do not manufacture urgency.

CTA: Comment `STATEMENT` for the redacted review checklist.

### Reel 3 — Approval is not activation

Frames:
1. APPROVAL — Underwriting accepted the account.
2. DEPLOYMENT — Equipment, gateway, or software is configured.
3. TRAINING — The merchant knows how to transact, batch, refund, and get support.
4. FIRST SALE — A live test verifies the system.
5. RETENTION — Confirm deposits, statements, and satisfaction after launch.

CTA: Comment `ACTIVATE` for the deployment checklist.

### Reel 4 — The agent field-ready system

Frames:
1. FUNDAMENTALS — Explain the payment flow without jargon.
2. OPENER — Complete a clear 20-second introduction.
3. DISCOVERY — Ask questions before presenting a solution.
4. MOCK CALL — Pass a scored practice conversation.
5. FIELD DAY — Complete and log real merchant attempts.

CTA: Comment `AGENT` for the field-ready scorecard.

## Funnel adaptation

→ HubSpot contact/company/deal

Every Reel must map to exactly one audience:

## What to learn from this reference

- Package expertise as a named operating system.
- Show artifacts and workflows, not only talking-head opinions.
- Sell organization, certainty, and a clear next step.
- Use personal content to create taste and trust, but connect it back to demonstrated competence.
- Build a library from concentrated filming rather than filming every day.
- Create one resource for each repeated CTA.

## What not to copy

- exact wording or numbering from the creator's Reels
- website compositions or animation
- toolkit documents, portal templates, proposals, contracts, welcome files, or paid resources
- creator photographs, B-roll, logos, fonts, music, or screenshots
- claims about premium clients or results that Targeted Market has not earned
- a client portal before actual merchant volume justifies one
