NO 01  thesis

find the money. fix it. get paid monthly.

the problem

$50k / mo merchant pays ~$1,000 in fees. the statement is built so they cannot read it. that is $12,000 a year, paid in pieces to a bank, a network, and a processor most owners cannot name.

the solve

dual pricing. legal. set up in one afternoon. their cost drops to ~$50 flat.

the residual

about 1 percent of processed volume routes to you. one $50k merchant = ~$500 / mo, illustrative. you do not return. you do not chase. you find the next merchant and you do it again.

NO 02  arithmetic

do the math.

ask for the statement. divide total fees by total volume. that is the real rate. show them what they pay. show them what they would pay with dual pricing. they decide.

"$50k / mo merchant. $1,000 in fees. dual pricing → $50 flat. they save $950. i earn ~$500 / mo while they process. one afternoon."

Illustrative example of how the model works. Not a promise of earnings. Actual residuals depend on the merchants you board, their volume, approval, pricing, and your effort. Results vary.
